What's happening

What Agriculture Victoria now requires from growers.

Since February 2025, all Victorian growers of leafy vegetables, berries, and melons must register with Agriculture Victoria and demonstrate they have a Food Safety Management System in place. Audits begin in 2027.

Good news: A 50% discount on registration fees applies in 2026. If you haven't registered yet, now is the best time — before the window closes and audits begin.

In practice, an auditor will want to see that you have records covering these areas:

The four core activities

What you need to record — and what good looks like.

Most growers are already doing these four things every week. The question is whether those records would hold up if someone asked to see them tomorrow. Here's what each activity needs to capture.

🧴
Spray & Chemical Use

Every chemical application needs a record — pesticide, herbicide, or fertiliser.

What a good record includes Product name & registration number · Dose & dilution rate · Date & time · Wind speed at time of application · Crop & area treated · Withholding period · Operator name
🌱
Planting

The starting point of your traceability chain. Without it, nothing else connects.

What a good record includes Variety · Quantity · Block, row, or bed · Date of planting · Seed / seedlings supplier · Responsible person
✂️
Harvesting

Links your planting records to delivery. This is where the traceability chain closes.

What a good record includes Product & variety · Block harvested · Quantity · Packaging type (crates, cartons, bins, boxes) · Date & time · Batch or lot number · Harvesting crew
🚛
Delivery & Cold Chain

Protects you if a customer raises a quality concern after produce leaves your farm.

What a good record includes Customer name & delivery address · Product & quantity · Batch number · Dispatch date · Temperature at dispatch · Vehicle or transport method

Common questions

Questions growers ask most.

Do I really need to register if I'm a small grower? ▾
Yes. The registration requirement applies to all growers of leafy vegetables, berries, and melons in Victoria regardless of size. There is no minimum acreage or volume threshold. If you grow these crops and sell them, you need to register.
Can I keep paper records or do I need a digital system? ▾
Paper records are accepted. However, they must be complete, legible, organised, and retrievable quickly for inspection. In practice, paper records are harder to search, easier to lose, and more difficult to use when demonstrating traceability. A simple digital system makes all of this significantly easier — but the choice is yours.
What is Freshcare and do I need to be accredited? ▾
Freshcare is an Australian on-farm food safety program widely recognised by retailers and wholesalers. Agriculture Victoria accepts Freshcare FSQ (Food Safety & Quality) accreditation as a recognised food safety management system. You are not required to be Freshcare accredited, but if your buyers require it, having your records in order makes the accreditation process much simpler.
When do audits start and what happens if I'm not ready? ▾
Audits are scheduled to begin in February 2027. Growers who are registered but cannot demonstrate a functioning food safety management system at audit time may face penalties or be required to rectify gaps within a set timeframe. The earlier you get organised, the less stressful the process will be.
What is traceability and why does it matter? ▾
Traceability means being able to follow any batch of produce — forward or backward — through every step it went through on your farm. If a customer calls about a delivery, you should be able to show where that product was planted, what chemicals were applied to it and when, when it was harvested, and when it was dispatched. If a food safety incident occurs and you can't demonstrate this, the consequences are much more serious.
